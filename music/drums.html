I like drums

And it's not just because I love hitting things.
I also play the guitar




I've been drumming since 5th grade, so you'd think that I'd be pretty good at it. That's what you get for thinking. Actually, I'm not half bad at it.
When I was given the opportunity to take band, our teacher asked each of us what instrument we wanted to play. I was told that I had good teeth for a trumpet player, so I wanted to do that. But my parents couldn't afford to buy me a trumpet, so I was forced to play percussion, like my older brother had 7 years earlier. I was bummed out at this time, but in hindsight it was probably the best thing that could have happened to me.
Eventually, my family moved to Provo UT, and that's where I was properly introduced to Rush! One of my friends in my Russian class was talking about them one day, so I asked him what album I should buy if I wanted to get a good representation of their music. He suggested that I listen to "Power Windows". I went out and bought it on tape, I was too poor at the time to pick it up on CD, and I was hooked. After that I went out and bought "Chronicles" on CD, and I listened to all of these songs that I had heard before and liked, but I had no idea that they were by Rush, I didn't even realize that they were all by the same artist. Remember that I grew up in a cultural black hole called Manson Washington. So my appreciation for Rush grew quickly, and since I had been a drummer for years by this point, I took a quick liking to Neil Peart. He is possibly the greatest rock drummer of all time. If you listen to a Rush song from the 2nd album on, and take note of the drumming, you will realize that this is not normal vanilla beat-keeping drumming. This is creative and interesting for a change. And if you try to emulate what you hear, you will realize that it takes more skill than you could have imagined possible.
Also, at Timpview high school in Provo, I was able to take drum line as one of my classes. This is where I learned a lot of drum technique.

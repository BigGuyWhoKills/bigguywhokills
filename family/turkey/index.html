Turkey

I spent a week in Turkey in August of 2004.  Here are some of the pictures from that trip.  I have over 200 pictures from this trip, so posted here are only a few of my favorites.


This wall was part of the old fortifications built about 2000 years ago.

I was totally going to steal that sword, but we were out of time, so I didn't.
You would be amazed at the things that we were not allowed to take pictures of in the palace.  I was an emerald the size of a hen's egg!  Not to mention all of the gold on display.  For several centuries the Ottoman and Byzantine empires had their capitals here in Constantinople (Istanbul).  This made the area effectively the capital of the world at that time.


The view from a restaurant that we ate at.  We were in Europe here, but across that river on the far bank, was the Asian part of Turkey.  Ankara is in Asia, so when we visited Ankara we hit the second continent of the trip (third if you count the layover in Europe).


This souffle was the best desert that I've had in my life ever!  It was served with a cream that you mix in with it, and if I wasn't already stuffed by all the swordfish that I had in the main course, I would have kept ordering these until..., heck, I'd still be in that restaurant now.


These aqueducts were built in Roman times, and are still functional though unused.  They feed the cisterns of Istanbul, one of which is shown a few images below.


The mosque's in Turkey are astounding!  Sorry but I didn't get the name of this one.


The largest chandelier that I've ever seen.  This was in the Hagia Sophia, and this area was being restored so it was off limits.  If the link doesn't work, search for "Hagia Sophia" on Google.


This is just one example of the beautiful mosaics in the Hagia Sophia.


This is the "Blue Mosque", a world famous attraction that is considered one of the most holy Muslim sites in the world.  This is (IMO) the most beautiful building in all of Turkey.  When it was built, many considered it sacrilegious because it was the first mosque to have the same number of minarets as Mecca.  Because of this it was built with it's defense in mind.  Sadly, we didn't have time to tour it.  This is another structure that you should Google for.


This is an Egyptian style obelisk built in the 3rd century located in the Hippodrome.  The was right next to the Blue Mosque and the Hagia Sophia.


This was were we had lunch on a small side street after visiting the Hagia Sophia.


This is the Basilica Cistern under Istanbul.  The cisterns were used to keep water stored without evaporating, and were fed water by the aqueducts that are shown a few images above.  The James Bond film "From Russia With Love" and Jackie Chan's "The Accidental Spy" had scenes shot here.


Andrew, Adam (me), and John in the cistern.


A local police officer that we got directions from.  I think it's cool that they all have full-auto MP5's.  Even bank guards have full auto MP5's.  Heckler and Koch must love the business.  I was being sly when I took this picture because I didn't know how he would feel about having his picture taken.


This is the view inside the Grand Bazaar in central Istanbul.  There are about 4000 shops in here, and over 12000 employees.  It's a small city unto itself.  A lot of rug shops and jewelers.  This is where I got most of my souvenirs and gifts for family members.  Don't get lost.  There are dozens of "streets" in here, but I was separated from my companions once, and had to meet up at the hotel.


A picture of one of the old streets just outside the Grand Bazaar.  The main roads in Istanbul are much more modern obviously, but there are still plenty of cool back roads like this one.


Another very cool looking mosque.  I seriously love the look of these buildings.


In Ankara we found another amazing Mosque (I failed to get the name of this one), but couldn't find a good angle to get a picture from, so I took a "bank shot" in the reflection of a nearby building.  I am seriously the coolest person I know.

United Arab Emirates

I spent a week (um, well, a week and a half) in the U.A.E. in 2004.  This is a photolog of the trip.

The sunrises in the Middle East are amazing:


Here's a decent picture of the hotel that I stayed at in Sharja:


Fast food is always available, even familiar fast food.  I didn't ever try Domino's, but I had Chili's twice, and it was good (a Mitch Hedberg reference).


Another sunrise, more magnificent than our sunsets here in Utah.


Near the customer's site we would sometimes encounter camels that had wandered from a nearby village.  Camels are slow and will not always move for automobiles, so you have to drive carefully around them.


This is what the sandstorms are like during the day.


This is the entrance to our customers site.


The desert out here is nothing like the deserts of the western United States.  This picture was taken from the bus that I rode to and back from the site.


Even the overpasses out here are amazingly beautiful.  Taken through the bus window and the quality suffers for it.


I wish I had taken this picture 400 milliseconds sooner.  As you can kind of tell, the traffic circles have some awesome architecture.


The guys that I went out to train.


This water fountain would spray water a good 200+ feet in the air.  Anyone want to do the calculations on how much energy that would take?


This is a riverside walkway under one of the overpasses near my hotel in Sharja.  It was very clean and very beautiful.  I was trying to get a good shot of the mosque down the way.


The same overpass but obviously from the sidewalk over the river.


Some cabbie is off-roading to get around the traffic slowed by the sandstorm.


As you can tell, it's possible to navigate without learning Arabic.  Of course, I have no idea what a "Corniche" is, other than guesses from the picture here, and neither does my spell-checker.


I'd love to go back and visit again.  Even the hassle of losing my passport wasn't too bad.  I figure that it only took a day longer than it would have taken had I lost my passport in England where there would be no (well, little) language barrier.  And heck, how many Americans can say they have a police report on them in Arabic?  I should scan that and post it here.

The best thing about my car is that it sounds real good.  No, not the engine, the stereo.  That reminds me, I need to get the engine fixed.  But the stereo is nice.  It's not setup to compete with the thump thump boys you hear around town, but my music sounds good (better than many thump systems), and I don't set off car alarms 2 blocks away.

My head unit is a Pioneer DEH-31 (35x4).  I know that I need to replace this, but my opinion is that decks are just on the verge of being exactly what I want. Here is a list of features that I dream about:
  • A large, non volatile storage device (at least 1 TB), preferably solid state memory.
  • It should be able to read all popular media from Bluray to CD, Compact Flash, SD, etc..., and be able to upgrade it's firmware to play new audio and video formats.
  • BlueTooth phone interface to pause/mute when a phone call comes in, and give the option of hands-free use through the stereo speakers and a microphone.
  • 802.11n or BlueTooth networking so I can add tracks from inside the house.  The car stereo should show up on the network just like any other shared storage device on the network.
  • It should have a simple color touch screen large enough to be useful for GPS navigation and basic network functions.  The 802.11 adapter will allow you to perform those functions (like simple web browsing, FTP, telnet, etc...) remotely.
  • A detachable face that doubles as a PMP with enough storage to be used as a sneakernet method of adding tracks.
  • More features to come...

I really want one of the Eclipse decks. Maybe I will buy one of those while I wait for the industry to come out with a deck that has the features that I crave.


(DEH-36 pictured)
I will replace this when decks get noticeably better.  I know, I know, MP3 is a great reason to upgrade, but I own over 500 CD's so I don't really need MP3 disks.

From there I send my speaker level outputs to a set of 4" Infinity Reference (4002i) series dash speakers.


The pre-amp output from the deck goes to a Coustic XM6 crossover.


From there I move the lower frequencies to my new (Feb 2004) Coustic 801DB (800 Watt RMS) Amplifier, and my mids and highs to my Pioneer GM-X262 200 watt amplifier.
.

The 801DB drives my Infinity Perfect 12 subwoofer (4 ohm, SVC) from 20Hz to 150Hz, and the XM-262 drives my rear deck of Infinity Reference 6.5" (6002i) speakers from 100Hz to 300Hz.
.

All in all, the balance is great! The system can thump, but I am trying to get it set it up for fidelity.

I've put in a false floor in the trunk of the Audi.  This protects my system from all the stuff that I put in my trunk.  Next I will amplify all of the full range speakers with Coustic amps.  I will probably use a Coustic 481QE.

Now, some pics of my car stereo install in my old Audi Model 80:


This was the first set of quality battery terminals that I was using on my Audi.  They are StreetWire terminals, and in this picture they are just running 8 gauge positive (Yes, for the entire car, engine and all.  Don't laugh, I am not well adjusted.) and 4 gauge ground.


This picture shows both terminals.  Notice how close the positive terminal is to the frame.  That's unsafe, even though the battery was very secure.  Later I replaced this with a fused battery terminal (see below).


This is the false floor in place, with just my sub in the trunk.


This is my first electronics board.  At this time I didn't have my 801DB, so I made a board out of some scrap particle board that I had left over from cutting the false floor.


Here is the false floor in place.  My electronics are totally protected from all of these tools and anything else I put in here.

.
This is my new electronics board (4 April 2004).  My nephew Ryan and I put together this setup in an afternoon, carpet and all.  This components are a Coustic XM6, a Pioneer GM-X262, and a Coustic 801DB.
Power comes in on 0 gauge wire on the left side of the chassis and signal comes in on 20' Monster Cable on the right side of the chassis.  Ground is currently 4 gauge, but I will change that to 0 gauge when I have bought the proper grounding lug.  All power from the distribution blocks run under the board, and all signal between devices runs under the board as well (avoiding other lines, or crossing at 90º where possible).


Here is a shot of my Infinity Perfect 12 (4 ohm, SVC) with my system in the background.


I just love this Phoenix Gold battery terminal!  It has two 0 gauge outputs and a single 4 gauge output, so I can easily hook up everything in my car.  It is also fused right on the terminal, making it extremely safe.  The negative terminal is a 4 gauge Streetwires, which I will swap out with a Phoenix Gold 0 gauge soon.


Here is my amp rack as of 15 May 2004.  All pre-amp signal is now transferred over Monster Cable THX RCA's.  The only thing I want to add to this is a replacement for the Pioneer amp.


I love having both of these Phoenix Gold battery terminals!  I also have a PG 1/0 gauge grounding lug (and a matching lug in the trunk).  You can see that I replaced the 4 gauge StreetWires terminal.  I will shorten this ground wire by a few inches so it doesn't pose any interference threat to the throttle linkage.  Then I need to run the positive wire through some other route, because this is too close to both the coil and the distributor.  I think those vents just behind it might be the best route (I don't recommend to anyone that they block ANY vent in their car).


This is my trunk without my tools inside.  I carpeted the false floor and used 2x4's to stiffen it so it no longer sags under the weight of my tools and sub.  You can also see my Monster Cable "Flex Pins" going into the Q-Logic 12" box.  These are a much better solution than just bare wire.

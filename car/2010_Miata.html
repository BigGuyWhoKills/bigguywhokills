2010-09 Miata paint job

My father and I started serious work on the Miata's paint job on September 8th of 2010, and finished on September 17th.  The reason that it took so long had mostly to do with a very casual sanding schedule, and a little bit of sub-optimal weather.  As I'm typing this up, the car is drying in the sun (we painted outside, as you will see in pictures soon).  I'm not making thumbnails for the pictures, just constraining them, so your load time may suck.  If you want thumbnails, decrease the width of your browser and the images will reduce in size (they are set to 80% of your window width).

Prior to this, I had done some light sanding, and had covered most bare metal with spray can primer.  To save money we continued to use that same spray can primer until the day of the shoot.  Here is some of the early prep work:

I need some new seats or good covers.  You can see my reflection in the rear fender.  That fender was painted separately from the rest of the car and had a much nicer finish.


2010-09-09:








2010-09-14: We had most of the sanding done here, and I was starting to mask off the windows and top.


Our masking tape (3M) was horrible for this job.  As it heated it would shrink.  The side that had better adhesion would pull the opposite side off.  This was an issue with sharp corners, like the upper right and upper left corners of the windshield.  It adhered to the glass better than it did to the rubber seal.










2010-09-16:








Fully masked off and nearly ready to paint:


I masked the doors separate from the top so they were open-able while the inside was properly sealed from overspray.






On the day of the shoot (2010-09-17), we used automotive grade primer sprayed from my father's gravity-feed SATAjet (not sure of the model, but it was made in West Germany if that's any indication of how long my dad has had this gun).  My dad is old-school, and can do almost anything.  He's also better than your dad.



With the car one solid color again, it looks so much better than patchwork color and primer.






You can start seeing irregularities and spots that we should have sanded better.  We did not intend for this to be a show car (evident by our spraying outdoors).








Then we sprayed the color.  Color is fun to watch go on.  This is where it starts looking like a car again.






The first coat is done.






In this shot you can see some blemishes just to the left of the sun's reflection.  Some are dust, some are bugs, but there are a few small lines that either did not fill, or that the paint did not take to.


For the last (4th) coat, dad used 50% paint and 50% mixing clear to get a MUCH better shine and add depth to the paint (without the cost and time associated with a basecoat/clearcoat job).  I will post pictures of that when I've taken the masking off.

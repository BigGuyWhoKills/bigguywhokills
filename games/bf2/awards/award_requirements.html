You can find a more updated guide here.  However, that page is often down.

On that site is a much more detailed guide, however a lot of the ribbons are kinda vague because few people can remember how they got them.  This was written from experience and everyone seems to have it slightly different, ESPECIALLY the ribbons. So, sorry if the guide does not help you out!

For a full list of the Ranks see my rank guide.

There is often more than one way to get the awards.  One is TIME related, another is KILL related, or it may be a combination of the two.


--Medals--

There are 18 unique medals, and each may be awarded multiple times.


Bronze Star : Awarded for placing 3rd overall in a round.




Silver Star : Awarded for placing 2nd overall in a round.




Gold Star : Awarded for placing 1st overall in a round.




Purple Heart : You need 20 Deaths and 5 kills in 1 round. (Deaths have to be gained first and must be 4x your kills, but minimum of 5 kills.)




--Badges--

There are 20 badge types, each with 3 ranks: Basic, Veteran, and Expert, for a total of 60 badges.


Basic Engineer Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Sniper Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Medic Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Spec-Ops Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Assault Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Anti-Tank Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Support Combat : 10 Kills in 1 round.




Basic Knife Combat : 7 Kills in 1 round.




Veteran Knife Combat : 57-80 Kills Total with Knife, 1 hour Total Knife use, and then get 7 kills in 1 round to get the badge.  The total number of kills seems to be variable, and it is currently unknown what governs this variable.




Basic Pistol Combat : 7 Kills in 1 round.




Veteran Pistol Combat : 57-80 Kills Total with Pistol, 1 hour Total Pistol use, and then get 7 kills in 1 round to get the badge.  The total number of kills seems to be variable, and it is currently unknown what governs this variable.




Basic Ground Defense : ~24 mins using TOW/MG position, or 10 kills, in 1 round.




Basic Explosive Ordinance : ~15 kills (With C4/AT mines/Claymores), in 1 round




Basic Commander : 40 Commander points in 1 round, and/or 20 kills, in 1 round.




Basic Engineer : ~5 Repair Points, in 1 round.




Basic First Aid : ~5 Heal Points (NOT Revive points), in 1 round.




Basic Re-Supply : ~5 Ammo Giving Points, in 1 round.




Basic Air Defense : ~24 mins using Stinger, or 10 kills, in 1 round.




Basic Armour : ~24 mins (In a Tank/APC), or 10 kills, in 1 round.




Basic Aviator : ~24 mins (In a plane), or 10 kills, in 1 round.




Basic Helicopter : ~24 mins (In a Chopper), or 10 kills, in 1 round.




Basic Transport : ~24 mins in Hummer/Jeep/ATV, or 10 kills, in 1 round.




--Ribbons--

These are awarded based on either time or performance. The scale is, Satisfactory, Good, Skilled, Outstanding, and Excellent. There may be other factors that we are not aware of them at this time.


Aerial Service Ribbon : 20 Kills in a Plane, and 1+ hours Total Plane use.




Air Defense Ribbon : ~24 mins in Stinger/AA, in 1 round. And/or 7 kills in Stinger/AA in same round.




Airborne Ribbon : Parachute 500m.




Combat Action Ribbon : Kills with kit or vehicle = 100+ Total? and, Time spent using kit or vehicle = 2+ hours?




Crew Service Ribbon : Driver Rating = ~100? and, Combat Rating = ~100 kills total?




Distinguished Service Ribbon : Time as Squad Leader = 10 hours, Time as Squad Member = 10 hours, Time as Commander = 10 hours and Total Teamwork Score = ~1,000.




Helicopter Service Ribbon : 20 Kills in 1 Round, and 1+ hours Total Heli Use.




Infantry Officer Ribbon : Squad Score = 100 in 1 round, Time as squad leader = 2+ hours Total.




Meritorious Unit Ribbon : Time as Squad Member = 10 hours Total, Teamwork Score = 40 in 1 round. (NOTE: You must play as a Medic/Engineer/Support in a Squad to get this award.)




Staff Officer Ribbon : 50 Commander points, in 1 round.




If any values are wrong, then they're wrong.  Without an official release from EA, no-one is going to be certain of anything.  As far as can be determined, promotions seem to be different for everyone, but the awards are given according to set criteria.

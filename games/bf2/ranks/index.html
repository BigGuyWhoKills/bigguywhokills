Understanding the Ranks

Battlefield 2's ranking system allows you to gain rank as you accumulate points by playing on ranked servers. All points (teamwork, combat, and command) are included in the rank calculation, so feel free to play whatever class you like.

There are twelve known ranks:

1. Private -- 0 Points (Starting rank)
2. Private First Class -- 500 Points
3. Lance Corporal -- 1000 Points
4. Corporal -- 10,500 Points
5. Sergeant -- 25,000 Points
6. Staff Sergeant -- 50,000 Points
7. Gunnery Sergeant -- 75,000 Points
8. Master Sergeant and 9. First Sergeant -- 150,000 Points*
10. Master Gunnery Sergeant and 11. Sergeant Major -- 250,000 Points*
12. Sergeant Major of the Corps -- 2,500,000 Points

Upon reaching the rank of Lance Corporal (1000 points), you'll be able to choose your first unlockable weapon. You can unlock any weapon no matter which class you played -- for example, you can earn all your points as a Medic and then choose to unlock the M-95 sniper rifle.

Players with higher rank higher rank will have priority when they apply for Commander position in a match. Also, the highest-ranked player will take control of a squad if its leader leaves.

*Note: In the United States Marine Corps ranking system (which BF2's is based upon), Master Sergeant and First Sergeant are within the same paygrade.  Likewise for Master Gunnery Sergeant and Sergeant Major.  This would explain why they share the same point values, although how the game decides which one you get promoted to is still unknown.  Dan offers the following hypothesis:

...in the army (and I know the game is based upon the USMC), a person starts as a Private and when they are ready for promotion, they either go to Corporal OR Specialist.  They are the same pay-grade, but a Specialist might go to a resupply unit, a maintenance unit, or some other important job that is not directly command related although they have the same "rank."  The corporal would be trained in the actual command of troops in combat and be on the "command track."

Now once these two soldiers move to NCO status, I'm not sure how it goes, but my guess would be that a person who receives the "Sergeant Major" rank is someone who got a lot of their points as a commander in the game, while someone who gets the "Master Gunnery Sergeant" rank would be someone who's been slogging it out on the ground in a squad.  I would imagine the game keeps track of the difference and assigns the rank accordingly.

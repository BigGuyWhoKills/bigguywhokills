Stories

My first solo PvP success - I've had a few good days lately.  This last weekend (2005-02-05) I also had my first PvP success all by myself (I had many PvP kills prior to this that I had gotten when part of a group).
I was walking back from the Ruins of Alterac (that I had no right being in because I was about 7 levels too low for it) when I saw a lvl 37 (I was lvl 32 at that time) Undead Warrior about to finish off an Ogre.  Since her health was about 80%, and I was certain that she had seen me already, I decided to take her head on.
As I ran towards her I turned on "Seal of the Crusader" and used "Judgment" to inflict her with that seal.  Both my "Devotion Aura" and my "Blessing of Might" were already enabled on me.  Once Seal of the Crusader was judged on her, I used "Seal of Command" to enable massive amounts of "Holy damage" against her.
She ran circles around me in order to try to attack me from behind and keep me from hitting her, but it wasn't enough, and I was able to keep her in front of me almost the entire time.  Her attacks were powerful, and it took less than 20 seconds for her to get my health down to 30%.  So I healed up and kept fighting.  With her health at about 65% I realized that I would need to heal a few times in order to defeat her.  With my Paladin healing and 6.9% chance of getting a critical hit, I was able to kill her and still had about 20% of my mana left (about 2 more heals if I needed them).

So I keep walking back to Southshore, stopping from time to time for beast kills, when who did I see at the top of a nearby hill?  That's right, our favorite undead warrior.  This time she was at full health and seemed to be pissed off.
So she charged down the hill at me, while I refreshed my Blessing of Might and turned on Seal of the Crusader again (My Devotion Aura was still on).  As she got within range I judged Seal of the Crusader on her and used Seal of Command again.  Again she ran circled around me again, but I was able to keep her in front of me the entire time.
Once my health was below 40% I decided to heal myself.  So I used "Hammer of Justice" to stun her for 4 seconds while I tended my wounds.  That restored me to about 70% health, and she was no longer stunned, so we started back at it.  She hadn't figured out by this time that the running in circles wasn't gonna work, and she began doing that again.  I knew it was just a matter of time before I win, and after a few more seconds I healed up again.  After the second heal, she must have figured out that it wasn't going well for her, and when she gets to about 20% health, she tries to run away.
But by this time my Hammer of Justice had recharged and I stunned her for another 4 seconds and continued to pound her mercilessly.  She recovered from that stun with about 7% health and continued to run in a panic.  I knew that it was a panic because she ran right into and past a tiger, and then she had two enemies to deal with.  She didn't last long then, and as a bonus to me, we had run past the tiger's hunting area, so when we killed her, the tiger didn't even attack me, but ran back to where he was before.
Now I had beaten a lvl 37 Warrior (five lvls above me) twice in about 20 minutes, so of course I broke out in dance and took a screenshot.
I will grant that each time I had a bit of help.  The first time she was at only 80% health from fighting that Ogre, and the second time the tiger added insult to injury, but I would take her on any day of the week.

2014 insight: It took me a very long time to kill that Warrior.  Paladin DPS was pathetic in 2005.  But the fact that I killed a Warrior, 5 levels my senior, two times, is a credit to the resilience of the Paladin class.  I also now realize she night not have been trying to get behind me to avoid my swings, but possibly to get a better hit table.  I didn't know about hit tables back then.  Also note that Paladin abilities are nothing like this anymore.

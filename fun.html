Look at all the great things to do in Provo / Orem!

Unfortunately there is practically nothing to do in this valley. After you have been here for a month or so it seems that you've done it all. But we can't give up without a fight, so here are some options that you may not have considered.

First, you can always count on a movie to numb the brain for an hour or two, so check out Deseret News.

Another way to spend time and money was playing paintball at Predator Paintball.  I'm sorry, but they have gone out of business, and that is a real shame.  This was probably the best couple of bucks you could spend in the valley.  For your money, they would equip you with a gun, 50 paintballs, protective gear, and a short instruction course focusing on safety, and the rules regarding 'kills'.  You could buy more than the original 50 paintballs that they gave you (you would get 50 for each hour you rent the equipment), but it could get expensive.  Example: My friend and I went in one time, and at the end of the day he had spent about $25 (for 3 hours), whereas I spent $65.  The difference, he was very careful with his ammo, and I went nuts with mine.  I still play paintball.  Now I own a Piranha STS G2, running Nitrous through a remote.

You can also pick up some RC equipment and go flying or boating (or cars, if you're afraid of a real challenge).  I fly a Thunder Tiger Raptor .30 helicopter.
